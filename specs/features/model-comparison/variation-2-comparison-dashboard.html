Concept

Comparison gets a dedicated full-page view, reached from a third top-level tab next to the two model tabs. One scrolling dashboard holds everything: model identity cards, headline totals with deltas, a "what explains the difference" decomposition, the compared charts, paired Sankeys, and the assumptions diff. Per-model results panels stay exactly as today inside each model's workspace — the dashboard is additive, a report-like surface you could walk a stakeholder through top to bottom.

Assessment

StrengthsRoom for everything — wide charts, paired Sankeys, the diff table; a single scrolling narrative that doubles as a stakeholder-facing report; per-model workspaces stay untouched; each existing Sankey analysis card generalizes naturally to a pair driven by one settings form.
WeaknessesA third top-level surface to maintain and keep consistent with per-model results; risk of dashboard sprawl as blocks accumulate; some content duplicated with per-model panels (same charts, compare flavour).
Best whenComparison is a first-class deliverable: studied, discussed, shown to others — not just glanced at while editing.
